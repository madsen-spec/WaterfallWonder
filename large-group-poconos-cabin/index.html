House flow

What helps a 12-person weekend run smoothly.

A group stay is not only about bed count. It also needs places to gather, split off, reset, and avoid turning every meal or activity into a drive.

Sleeping

Confirm bed assignments early

The site baseline is 2 king beds, 4 queen beds, 1 queen-size sleeper sofa with a queen mattress, and 3 bathrooms for up to 12 guests. Use the sleeping layout guide and booking page before assigning couples, kids, or late arrivals.

Game room

Give the group a second center

The game room helps full-house stays because not everyone has to be in the same living area at the same time.

Meals

Use the kitchen to keep the day moving

Large groups move better when groceries, snacks, coffee, and dinner plans are settled before the first big outing.

Common areas

Give the group indoor and outdoor options

Use the living room, decks, screened areas, hot tub, and fire pit as separate places to gather, subject to weather, house rules, and seasonal restrictions.

Parking

Confirm vehicle instructions

Before arrival, decide how many vehicles are coming, where guests should park, and whether Saw Creek or weather conditions affect parking or access.

Quiet hours

Plan noise before night one

Quiet hours are part of the house rules. For larger groups, talk through hot tub, fire pit, and game room use before the first night.

Before you book

Sort out the basics before arrival.

Most group-trip stress comes from practical details. Handle them before arrival and the stay feels easier. The safety and access notes collect the shared rules guests should understand before everyone arrives.

Guest count

Confirm the final number

Make sure the guest count fits the booking limit, including how the group treats infants, late arrivals, and anyone who may only visit during the day.

Sleeping

Assign rooms before arrival

Review the king beds, queen beds, sleeper sofa, bedrooms, bathrooms, and platform bed-count display before assigning couples, kids, early sleepers, or late-night guests.

Vehicles

Plan parking as a group

Count vehicles, share arrival timing, and avoid assuming overflow or street parking without property-specific guidance.

Meals

Decide food before the outing

Set the grocery, coffee, snack, restaurant, and dinner plan early so the kitchen helps the weekend instead of becoming a bottleneck.

Pets

Handle dog approval early

If dogs are joining, secure approval and review fees, cleanup responsibilities, furniture rules, and hot tub restrictions before the group assumes the plan is set.

Amenities

Understand Saw Creek access

Community registration, amenity access, guest names, passes, hours, fees, and seasonal availability are controlled by Saw Creek rules.

FAQ

Large-group stay questions.

How many guests can stay at Waterfall Wonder?

The public listing baseline is up to 12 guests, not including infants. Review the booking page before reserving.

How many beds and bathrooms are listed?

This site presents 2 king beds, 4 queen beds, 1 queen-size sleeper sofa with a queen mattress, and 3 bathrooms. Review room-by-room placement on WanderHome before booking because platform displays can be updated.

Is there enough to do at the house for a group?

Yes. The house is positioned around waterfall views, a game room, hot tub, fire pit, decks, kitchen and dining spaces, and common areas for cabin downtime.

What should we review about parking?

Review vehicle count, parking instructions, winter road conditions, and any community access details before arrival. Do not assume overflow or street parking without property guidance.

Where should large groups review policies?

Use WanderHome for the calendar, booking policies, room layout, parking guidance, Saw Creek registration, pet approvals, and house rules.
