The quick answer

Know the room plan before anyone calls a space.

Waterfall Wonder has four legal bedrooms, two open sleeping areas, and a queen sleeper sofa. The named spaces span the second, main, and lower floors.

Planning note

The room details on this page were confirmed by the owner. WanderHome remains the live booking destination for dates, fees, policies, and pet approval.

Room-by-room layout

Six named sleeping spaces, plus a queen sleeper sofa.

The Master, Deer, Bear, and Raccoon rooms are legal bedrooms. The Loft and Cove are open sleeping areas with different privacy arrangements. The queen sleeper sofa is the seventh sleeping option; its location is not stated here.

Before arrival

Decide these four things before the cars pull in.

Sleeping

Assign the four legal bedrooms, two open sleeping areas, and queen sleeper sofa before arrival.

Bathrooms

The Master has an en suite bathroom that the Loft also shares. The Deer Room is across from the main-floor bathroom; the Bear and Raccoon rooms are across from the lower-level bathroom, which is also near the Cove.

Access

Every exterior entrance requires steps, and the route between the home and driveway also uses steps. Exact step counts are not stated.

Privacy

The Loft uses a removable privacy wall and the Cove uses privacy curtains. Decide who is comfortable in the open sleeping areas.

Bathrooms and access

Know which bathroom serves each floor.

The Master has an en suite bathroom, the main-floor bathroom is across from the Deer Room, and the lower-level bathroom is across from the Bear and Raccoon rooms and near the Cove.

FAQ

Sleeping layout questions.

How many guests can Waterfall Wonder sleep?

Waterfall Wonder is presented for up to 12 guests with 2 king beds, 4 queen beds, a queen sleeper sofa, and 3 bathrooms. WanderHome remains the live booking destination for current policies and stay terms.

Where are the beds located?

The second floor has the Master Bedroom with a king and the open Loft Sleeping Area with a queen. The main floor has the Deer Room with a queen. The lower level has the Bear and Raccoon Rooms with queens and the open Cove Sleeping Area with a king. The sleeper sofa is queen-size.

Should groups assign rooms before arrival?

Yes. Four legal bedrooms and two open sleeping areas have different bathroom and privacy arrangements, so groups should assign spaces before arrival.
