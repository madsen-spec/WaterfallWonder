Quick answer

The quick answer: Bushkill Falls is the easiest public waterfall recommendation; Dingmans needs a live status check.

Bushkill Falls is usually the easiest public waterfall to recommend when the park is open. Dingmans Falls is still important for planning, but NPS current conditions require a prominent check-before-visiting note. Raymondskill Falls is a strong scenic add-on for guests heading toward Milford.

Planning notes

Waterfall planning notes

After rain

Waterfalls can be more dramatic, but trails and wooden surfaces may be slick. Choose footwear accordingly.

Peak weekends

Arrive early and have a second plan. Parking and trailhead access can fill or change quickly.

Winter

Treat waterfall trails as conditions-dependent. Ice can make short trails feel much more serious.

FAQ

Waterfall planning questions.

Can guests walk down to the property waterfall?

Guests should treat the property waterfall as a view and setting, not as a public trail. The natural terrain is steep and guest guidance should be followed.

Is Dingmans Falls open?

As of the May 8, 2026 review, NPS current conditions still required a closure warning for Dingmans Falls access. Check the official NPS page before visiting.

Which waterfall is easiest to recommend?

Bushkill Falls is usually the easiest guest recommendation when the private park is operating, because the attraction manages its own trails and visitor information.
